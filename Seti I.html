Seti I

Seti I was the second pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty of Egypt. He is known for his military campaigns and for being one of the most significant pharaohs of ancient Egypt...

Seti I worked to restore Egypt’s borders to their former extent, focusing particularly on the regions of Canaan, Syria, and Nubia. His reign is considered one of the high points of the Nineteenth Dynasty...

He is known for building an impressive series of temples, including the Temple of Abydos, one of the most important religious sites in ancient Egypt...

Seti I's reign was marked by peace and stability within Egypt, although he also had to deal with internal revolts and incursions from foreign enemies...

His tomb in the Valley of the Kings is one of the most elaborately decorated tombs in the valley, and it features some of the finest wall reliefs from ancient Egyptian art...
