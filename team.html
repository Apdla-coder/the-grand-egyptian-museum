Museum Management

Management of the Grand Egyptian Museum

The management of the Grand Egyptian Museum oversees the planning, operation, and development of the museum to ensure it becomes a global hub of heritage, culture, and innovation.

Our Vision

To position the Grand Egyptian Museum as the leading cultural destination showcasing the legacy of ancient Egypt through advanced museological practices.

Our Mission

To preserve, research, and present Egypt’s heritage in an engaging and sustainable way, while promoting cultural education and tourism.

Key Objectives

  • Preserve and display over 100,000 artifacts.
  • Implement smart technology in exhibits and operations.
  • Create educational programs for global audiences.
  • Collaborate with international museums and institutions.

Directors of the Grand Egyptian Museum

Dr. Tarek Tawfik

Dr. Tarek Tawfik is the General Supervisor of the Grand Egyptian Museum. He has an extensive background in Egyptology and museum management. Dr. Tawfik has been a key figure in the museum's development, overseeing its design, collections, and exhibition strategies.

  • Supervised the museum’s design and layout.
  • Key figure in exhibition strategies.

Dr. Zahi Hawass

"Dr. Zahi Hawass is a renowned archaeologist and former Minister of Antiquities in Egypt. He is best known for his work on the Valley of the Kings and his leadership in Egyptian archaeological research. Dr. Hawass contributed greatly to the museum's vision and was instrumental in its early planning stages.", Major General Atef Moftah"
