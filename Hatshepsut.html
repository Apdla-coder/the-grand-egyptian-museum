Hatshepsut

Hatshepsut was the fifth pharaoh of the Eighteenth Dynasty of Egypt. She is one of the most successful female pharaohs in ancient Egyptian history and is best known for her architectural achievements and long reign...

Hatshepsut's reign is noted for its period of peace and prosperity. She is recognized for expanding Egypt's trade networks, particularly with the Land of Punt, which brought vast wealth to Egypt...

She is also known for constructing some of Egypt's most iconic monuments, including her mortuary temple at Deir el-Bahari, which is considered a masterpiece of ancient Egyptian architecture...

During her reign, Hatshepsut focused on restoring temples and monuments throughout Egypt, strengthening the economy, and maintaining peace in the region...

Hatshepsut was depicted in many of her monuments wearing the traditional male regalia of a pharaoh, including a false beard, which was unusual for a female ruler...
